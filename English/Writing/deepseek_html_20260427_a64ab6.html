Creative Writing: Zoom-In, Show Don't Tell & Word Power

🎯 Interactive: figurative language • sensory details • sentence upgrades • fronted adverbials
📌 THE ZOOM-IN METHOD (Movie Camera)
🎬 Wide shot: whole scene (forest, city, haunted house)
🔍 Mid shot: main focus (a figure, a door, a tree)
🔎 Close-up: tiny detail (splintered wood, a trembling hand)
➕ Always mix wide → mid → close for immersive description.

📘 Level 1 – Easy (Zoom-In & Sensory Basics)

EASY 1 In the Zoom-In method, which shot describes "a single torn photograph on the mantelpiece"?
EASY 2 Which sense is used in: "the musty smell of rotting wood"?
EASY 3 Upgrade: "He walked slowly" → replace with ONE powerful verb.
EASY 4 Show 'fear' without saying 'scared' — write 3–4 words (e.g., "hands trembled")

📘 Level 2 – Intermediate (Figurative Language & Colour)

INTERMEDIATE 1 Identify: "The wind howled in rage" — simile, metaphor or personification?
INTERMEDIATE 2 Complete the simile: "The fog hung over the city like ___________" (2-4 words)
INTERMEDIATE 3 Which colour creates a cold, ominous atmosphere? (crimson / amber / indigo)
INTERMEDIATE 4 Write a metaphor for the sun (e.g., "the sun was a golden coin") — your turn.

📘 Level 3 – Advanced (Fronted Adverbials & Punctuation Power)

ADVANCED 1 Write a fronted adverbial of place (example: "At the edge of the forest,"). Type yours.
ADVANCED 2 Add a dash to create interruption: "I was about to open the door ______" (finish with dash)
ADVANCED 3 Which sentence uses ellipsis for suspense? A) "Help!" B) footsteps… closer… closer… C) "Run away!"
ADVANCED 4 Rewrite: "The sun went down" → use personification (give the sun a human action).
ADVANCED 5 Write a complex sentence starting with an '-ing' clause: "Running through the darkness, ..." (complete the sentence)

⚡ Level 4 – Challenging (Upgrade Drills & Editing)

CHALLENGING 1 Upgrade this weak sentence: "The house was old and scary." (use 2 sensory details)
CHALLENGING 2 Add a colon to introduce a dramatic detail: "He saw only one thing ___" (complete with colon)
CHALLENGING 3 Name three different senses you should use in a descriptive paragraph.
CHALLENGING 4 Write an upgraded version: "The wind was strong" → show it through action without using "strong".
CHALLENGING 5 What punctuation creates a dramatic pause and works well in horror writing? (dash / ellipsis / colon)

🎓 MASTERY TEST (12 mixed creative writing skills)

1. What is the first stage of the Zoom-In method? (wide / mid / close-up)
2. Write a simile to describe a silent character: "He was as silent as ____________"
3. Personification: "The moon ___________ over the sleeping city." (give human action)
4. Replace 'walked' with a stronger verb in: "He walked across the battlefield."
5. Which colour palette fits a haunted house? (amber/crimson/charcoal vs honeyed/gilded)
6. Write a fronted adverbial of time (example: "Before dawn,").
7. Ellipsis creates... (suspense / excitement / joy) – type the correct effect.
8. Show 'excitement' without saying excited (3–6 words).
9. Name one type of sensory detail that ISN'T sight.
10. Combine short sentences using a semicolon: "The rain fell. The streets flooded."
11. Which style of sentence builds tension? (long / short / medium)
12. Write a close-up detail from a storm scene (tiny focus, e.g., "a single raindrop sliding down glass").
