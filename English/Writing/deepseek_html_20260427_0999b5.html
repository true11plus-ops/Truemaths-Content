Planning, Drafting & Editing: The Full Cycle

🎯 5-Minute Plan • TiPToP Paragraphing • S.P.A.G. Search • Sentence Rhythm
📌 THE 5-MINUTE PLAN (The "Skeleton")
🧠 Mind Map: Setting → Character → Problem/Plot arc (1-2-3)
📝 Vobab Drop: Write 3–5 high-level words in the margin IMMEDIATELY
🔄 Circular check: Does the ending link back to the beginning?
⏱️ Time: 5 minutes max – then write without stopping.

📘 Level 1 – Easy (Planning & Paragraph Basics)

EASY 1 In the 5-minute plan, what should you do immediately after your mind map? (Vobab Drop / write first sentence / check spelling)
EASY 2 What does TiPToP stand for? (Time, Place, Topic, Person / Tone, Pitch, Theme, Paragraphs)
EASY 3 True or False: You should start a new paragraph every time you change location in a story.
EASY 4 In planning, a "circular structure" means the ending should ________ to the beginning. (link back / be completely different)

📘 Level 2 – Intermediate (Sentence Rhythm & Punctuation)

INTERMEDIATE 1 Identify the rhythm: "The rain hammered against the window. It wouldn't stop. It hadn't stopped for three days." — what pattern?
INTERMEDIATE 2 Add the missing comma: "Slowly the massive oak tree began to fall."
INTERMEDIATE 3 Which sentence opener is an '-ing' starter? A) Exhausted from the race, B) Running through the forest, C) Over the hill,
INTERMEDIATE 4 Dialogue minimalism: "I'm sad because my dog ran away," she explained. — Which part is unnecessary? (rewrite without telling emotion)

📘 Level 3 – Advanced (S.P.A.G. Search & Editing)

ADVANCED 1 Which word is misspelled? "The commitee made thier final decision." (type both corrections)
ADVANCED 2 Fix the homophone: "Your going to be late if you don't leave now."
ADVANCED 3 Fix tense shift: "Last night, I walk to the shop and bought some milk."
ADVANCED 4 Which S.P.A.G. element is wrong? "The bag of apples were on the counter." (Spelling / Punctuation / Agreement / Grammar)
ADVANCED 5 Upgrade the weak word: "The weather was bad." Replace 'bad' with a stronger adjective.

⚡ Level 4 – Challenging (Paragraphing & Editing Passages)

CHALLENGING 1 Where should the first paragraph break be? "The forest was silent. A twig snapped. Back in the village, his mother waited anxiously." (after which word?)
CHALLENGING 2 Edit this sentence for sentence opener variety (avoid starting with 'The'): "The sun was setting. The sky turned crimson. The birds returned to their nests." (rewrite as one or two sentences)
CHALLENGING 3 Find ALL errors: "Their are three reason's why we should act: firstly the environment; secondly our health and thirdly future generations." (type corrections)
CHALLENGING 4 Apply Vobab Drop: the prompt is "a haunted house". Write 3 high-level vocabulary words you'd use in your margin.
CHALLENGING 5 What is the 'Final Look' strategy? (describe in one sentence)

🎓 MASTERY TEST – Process Mastery (12 questions)

1. How many minutes should you spend planning a 30-minute writing task?
2. Name one change that triggers a new paragraph (TiPToP).
3. Fix the comma error: "After the storm the sun finally appeared."
4. What does 'Vobab Drop' mean in planning?
5. Correct the homophone: "Its to late to change you're mind now."
6. Which sentence rhythm creates tension? (long and flowing / short and punchy)
7. Upgrade 'said' to a more vivid verb: "I hate you," he said.
8. Fix tense: "Yesterday, I see a shooting star and made a wish."
9. What does 'S' in S.P.A.G. stand for?
10. Name one type of sentence opener other than 'The' or a character's name.
11. Should you edit during the drafting phase? (yes/no – and why)
12. What should be the very first sentence of your story act as? (hook / conclusion / description)
