D.A.F.O.R.E.S.T. Toolkit & The Art of Persuasion

🎯 Interactive: identify techniques • upgrade sentences • counter-argument • rhetorical devices
🔥 D.A.F.O.R.E.S.T. – The Persuasion Machine
D – Direct Address (you) A – Alliteration F – Facts O – Opinion as Truth R – Rhetorical Question E – Emotive Language S – Statistics T – Rule of Three
📌 Five-Paragraph Structure: Intro (hook + opinion) → Point 1 (fact/statistic) → Point 2 (emotive) → Counter-argument + Rebuttal → Conclusion (call to action)

📘 Level 1 – Easy (Identify D.A.F.O.R.E.S.T. Techniques)

EASY 1 Which technique is used? "You have the power to make a difference today."
EASY 2 Identify: "Is it fair that some children go to school hungry?"
EASY 3 "Safe, secure, sustainable streets for everyone" — which technique?
EASY 4 "This heartbreaking tragedy must never happen again" — name the technique.

📘 Level 2 – Intermediate (Multiple Techniques & Upgrades)

INTERMEDIATE 1 "9 out of 10 dentists recommend this toothpaste. Don't you want a brighter smile?" – name TWO techniques.
INTERMEDIATE 2 Upgrade weak persuasive sentence: "We should save the park because it's nice." Write a D.A.F.O.R.E.S.T. version.
INTERMEDIATE 3 Which high-level connective means 'for this reason'? (Furthermore / Consequently / However)
INTERMEDIATE 4 Write an imperative verb (bossy verb) to start a call to action. Example: "Act now!" → your verb.

📘 Level 3 – Advanced (Counter-Argument & Rhetorical Power)

ADVANCED 1 Complete the counter-argument starter: "Some may argue that school uniform is restrictive. However, ..." (write a rebuttal 1 sentence)
ADVANCED 2 What is anaphora? Choose: A) repetition at start of sentences B) rhetorical question C) rule of three
ADVANCED 3 Write a hypophora (ask a question, then answer it) about climate action.
ADVANCED 4 Which tone suits a letter to the headteacher? (passionate / formal / humorous)
ADVANCED 5 Identify the tricolon: "For our children, for our community, for our future — act now!"

⚡ Level 4 – Challenging (Persuasive Paragraphs & Tone Shift)

CHALLENGING 1 Write a persuasive opening sentence for a speech to classmates about recycling. Use a rhetorical question.
CHALLENGING 2 Rewrite this for formal tone (letter to council): "Hey, you guys really need to fix the library, it's a total mess!"
CHALLENGING 3 Write a rebuttal to this counter-argument: "Some people believe that school uniform costs too much for families."
CHALLENGING 4 Name two advanced persuasive techniques BEYOND D.A.F.O.R.E.S.T.
CHALLENGING 5 Write a powerful conclusion (2 sentences) for an argument against plastic pollution. Include a call to action.

🎓 MASTERY TEST – 12 Mixed Persuasive Questions

1. Identify technique: "What kind of future do we want to leave for our children?"
2. What does the 'F' in D.A.F.O.R.E.S.T. stand for?
3. Upgrade: "We should ban phones because they are bad." Use 'emotive language' and a 'statistic'.
4. Which connective introduces a contrasting point? (Furthermore / Conversely / Consequently)
5. Write a rule of three about protecting nature.
6. Complete the counter-argument: "Admittedly, change takes time. Nevertheless, ..."
7. Name the technique: "Act. Support. Donate. Whatever you can do today matters."
8. Write an imperative verb to start a fundraising appeal.
9. Which paragraph of the 5-paragraph essay should include a counter-argument?
10. Identify technique: "This disastrous decision will destroy our community."
11. What is hypophora? (ask & answer / repetition / three-part list)
12. Write a strong call to action (one sentence) for a speech about volunteering.
