⚠️ 2. Its vs. It's – The Apostrophe Trap

Its = possessive (belonging to it) – "The dog wagged its tail" (NO apostrophe)

It's = contraction of it is or it has – "It's raining outside" (apostrophe = missing letter)

📌 RULE: Possessive pronouns (its, yours, hers, ours, theirs) NEVER have apostrophes!

🧠 3. Substitution Method – Step-by-Step

Example 1: affect/effect
Sentence: "The medicine had a positive ______."
Step 1: Replace with "result" → "had a positive result" ✅ makes sense → effect (noun).
Step 2: Replace with "influence" (verb) → "had a positive influence" ❌ odd with "a" → effect is correct.

Example 2: stationary/stationery
Sentence: "I need new ______ for school."
Step 1: Think "envelopes" (has E) → stationery ✅ (paper products).
Step 2: "Not moving" (anchor – has A) → stationary ❌ here.

Example 3: your/you're
Sentence: "______ going to love this book."
Step 1: Replace with "you are" → "You are going to love" ✅ → you're.

Example 4: its/it's
Sentence: "The cat licked ______ paw."
Step 1: Replace with "it is" → "it is paw" ❌ nonsense → its (possessive).

🔄 4. Homophone vs. Homograph vs. Homonym

TypeDefinitionExample
HomophoneSame sound, different spelling, different meaningthere/their/they're
HomographSame spelling, different meaning (may sound different)lead (to guide) / lead (metal); wind (air) / wind (to twist)
HomonymSame spelling AND sound, different meaningbat (animal) / bat (sports equipment)
