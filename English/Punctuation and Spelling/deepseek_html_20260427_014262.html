Hyphens, Dashes & Brackets:
Complete Exam-Ready Guide

✔ Covers ALL GL traps: numbers, ages, suspended hyphens, en/em dashes, square brackets, mixed punctuation + 30+ practice tasks

➖ Hyphen (-) — The Joiner

🔹 Core mission: Hyphens join words to avoid ambiguity, create compound adjectives before nouns, and clarify meaning.

✨ 1. Compound adjectives BEFORE a noun (major 11+ rule)

✔ a well-known actor
❌ the actor is well-known (wrong after noun) → well known

🔢 2. Numbers 21–99 (always hyphenate)

twenty-one • thirty-four • ninety-nine • one hundred and twenty-two

🧩 3. Fractions as adjectives

✔ a two-thirds majority  |  ✔ three-quarters of the cake (fraction noun = no hyphen? only when adjective)
⚠️ Important: “two thirds of the population” (no hyphen – it's a noun) vs “a two-thirds share” (adjective + noun → hyphen).

🎂 4. Ages as adjectives

✔ a ten-year-old girl  |  ✔ The building is ten years old (no hyphens after noun)

⏸️ 5. Suspended hyphens (GL favourite)

The first-, second-, and third-place winners  |  pre- and post-war era

👑 6. Prefixes before proper nouns & confusion avoiders

ex-president • pro-British • un-American • mid-September • re-sign (sign again) vs resign (quit)

📚 7. Compound nouns & double letters

mother-in-law • passer-by • check-in • re-elect • semi-independent
✨ “high school student” → no hyphen because “high school” is a noun phrase; “well-known author” needs hyphen.

— Dash (En dash – / Em dash —) — The Dramatic Pause

🔸 En dash (–) for ranges & scores. 🔸 Em dash (—) for breaks, emphasis, parenthesis (stronger than commas).

📊 En dash (–) ranges & connections

Pages 10–15 • 1990–2000 • London–Paris flight • score 3–2

💥 Em dash ( — ) – dramatic list or colon substitute

He had one passion — music.  |  She looked under the bed — nothing.

🖇️ Dashes in pairs (parenthesis)

The cat — a fluffy ginger — slept on the sofa. (dashes replace commas/brackets)

🗣️ Interrupted speech (GL comprehension passages)

“I was going to—” “No time!” she shouted.
⚠️ Overusing dashes loses marks. GL style: max 1–2 dashes per paragraph; use brackets for formal interruptions.

〔 〕 Brackets (Parentheses & Square Brackets) — The Removable Detail

✅ Removal test: If the sentence still makes grammatical sense, brackets are used correctly.

📌 Round brackets (parentheses)

The old house (built in 1703) finally collapsed.

🔲 Square brackets [ ] – editorial insertions

“It [the castle] was built in 1066.”  |  He said, “I am […] ready.”

🔢 Brackets for lists/numbers

You need (1) a pen, (2) paper, and (3) a ruler.
📌 Punctuation outside brackets unless full sentence inside: ✔ She arrived late (the train was delayed). ❌ (The train was delayed.) She arrived late.

📝 Mixed Punctuation – GL Exam Style

Q1. Choose the correctly punctuated sentence:




✔ B) The twenty-two-year-old athlete won. (hyphenated compound age adjective).

Q2. Which punctuation can replace the dashes in: The winner — a girl from York — celebrated. (choose all that apply)




✔ Commas and brackets both work: The winner, a girl from York, celebrated. / The winner (a girl from York) celebrated. Hyphen cannot replace paired dashes.

Q3. Insert the correct dash (en/em): The years 1999–2005 show growth. Which dash?



✔ En dash (–) for ranges: 1999–2005.

Q4. Which sentence uses brackets correctly?




✔ A) Square brackets [ ] are used to insert clarifying words inside a quotation.

Q5. Identify the error: He is a well known expert in pre and post-war architecture.

❌ Missing hyphens: well-known expert; suspended hyphens: pre- and post-war architecture.

✍️ Rewriting & Error Correction (Must-Know for GL)

🔧 Rewrite 1: Join the two ideas using a dash for dramatic effect: He opened the door. Nothing was there.

✔ He opened the door — nothing was there.

🔧 Rewrite 2: Correct the age hyphenation: A five years old girl sang beautifully.

✔ A five-year-old girl sang beautifully.

⚠️ Error spot 1: The range was 15 — 25 metres.

❌ Use en dash not em dash: 15–25 metres. (no spaces with en dash typical UK style: 15–25).

⚠️ Error spot 2 (brackets + punctuation): (The bus arrived late.) We missed the start.

❌ Brackets should not enclose a full sentence unless it's standalone. Rewrite: The bus arrived late (it was delayed), so we missed the start. Or remove parentheses.

📝 Insert one hyphen and one dash: The ex prime minister he was a controversial figure gave a speech.

✔ The ex-prime minister — he was a controversial figure — gave a speech.

🎯 Mark Scheme Insight: Hyphen, Dash or Bracket?

GL assessment preferences:
• Hyphens are mandatory in compound adjectives before nouns → losing hyphen = wrong.
• Dashes (em dash) for dramatic effect are acceptable, but overusing lowers style marks.
• Brackets are safest for asides; dashes add emphasis. Both pass removal test.
• En dash for ranges is tested in comprehension (dates, scores). Commas are incorrect there.
