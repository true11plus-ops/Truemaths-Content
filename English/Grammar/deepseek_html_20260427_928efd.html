🔎 Commas of Interruption – embedded relative clauses

Restrictive (essential) – no commas, can use 'that'.

The book that I read was good. (Which book? The one I read – essential)

Non‑restrictive (extra info) – commas needed, cannot use 'that'.

The book, which I read, was good. (Only one book – the reading is extra)

📋 Printable Cheat Sheet (A5 exam day)

Main clause: stands alone → "The owl hooted."
Subordinate clause: cannot stand alone → "because it was midnight"
ISAWACUBED: If, Since, Although/As, When/While/Wherever, After, Because, Until, Unless.
Comma rule: Subordinate clause FIRST → comma AFTER it.
Relative pronouns: who(subj) / whom(obj) / which(things) / whose(possession) / that(restrictive).
Restrictive: no commas, essential. Non‑restrictive: commas, extra info.
Omission rule: can omit relative pronoun only when it is the OBJECT.
