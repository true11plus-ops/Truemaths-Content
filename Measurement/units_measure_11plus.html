✏️ Worked Example 1 — Simple Conversion (cm → m)
Question: Convert 850 cm to metres.
Direction: cm → m means small to big → divide
Conversion factor: 100 cm = 1 m
Calculation: 850 ÷ 100 = 8.5
✅ 850 cm = 8.5 m
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Decimal Conversion (km → m)
Question: Convert 3.75 km to metres.
Direction: km → m means big to small → multiply
Calculation: 3.75 × 1000 = 3750
✅ 3.75 km = 3750 m
✏️ Worked Example 3 — Compound Jump (mm → km)
Question: Convert 3,500,000 mm to km.
Method A (two hops):
Step 1: mm → m: 3,500,000 ÷ 1,000 = 3,500 m
Step 2: m → km: 3,500 ÷ 1,000 = 3.5 km
Method B (direct): 3,500,000 ÷ 1,000,000 = 3.5 km
✅ 3,500,000 mm = 3.5 km
🏃 Context Problem — Running Track
Question: A running track is 400 m long. How many km is 6 laps?
Step 1: Total metres = 6 × 400 = 2,400 m
Step 2: m → km: 2,400 ÷ 1,000 = 2.4 km
✅ 6 laps = 2.4 km
💡 Mixed Units — Exam Trap

Questions like "2 m 45 cm" mean you must add 200 cm + 45 cm = 245 cm. Always convert to one unit before calculating.

4Time Units — Full Section
⚠️ Critical: Time Is NOT Base-10!

Every other metric conversion uses ×10 or ×1000. Time uses 60 (seconds↔minutes, minutes↔hours) and 24 (hours↔days). You cannot move a decimal point. Always write out the steps.

📐 Conversion Chain
60 seconds = 1 minute
60 minutes = 1 hour
24 hours = 1 day   ·   7 days = 1 week   ·   365 days = 1 year
📐 12-Hour ↔ 24-Hour Clock Rules
am times: 24hr = 12hr (no change). e.g. 08:20 = 8:20 am
pm times: 24hr = 12hr time + 12. e.g. 3:45 pm → 3:45 + 12:00 = 15:45
24hr → 12hr: if ≥ 13:00, subtract 12 and add pm. e.g. 19:30 → 7:30 pm
Midnight = 00:00   ·   Noon = 12:00
✏️ Worked Example 1 — Clock Conversion
Q: Convert 3:45 pm to 24-hour clock.
Rule: pm → add 12 to the hours
Calculation: 3 + 12 = 15 → 15:45
✅ 3:45 pm = 15:45
✏️ Worked Example 2 — Time Difference (No Borrowing)
Q: A train leaves at 09:47 and arrives at 12:15. How long is the journey?
Method — Count On:
09:47 → 10:00 = 13 minutes
10:00 → 12:00 = 2 hours
12:00 → 12:15 = 15 minutes
Total: 2 hours + 13 + 15 minutes = 2 hours 28 minutes
✅ Journey time = 2 hours 28 minutes
✏️ Worked Example 3 — Adding Time (Crossing Hours)
Q: A film starts at 7:35 pm and lasts 1 hour 55 minutes. When does it end?
Step 1: Add minutes: 35 + 55 = 90 minutes = 1 hour 30 minutes
Step 2: Add hours: 7 pm + 1 hour (from film) + 1 hour (from minutes) = 9 pm
Step 3: Add remaining 30 minutes: 9:00 pm + 0:30 = 9:30 pm
✅ Film ends at 9:30 pm (21:30)
⚠️ Common Traps

Borrowing across noon/midnight: If a journey crosses noon or midnight, be careful with am/pm switching. Use 24-hour clock to avoid errors.
Wrong subtraction: 12:15 − 09:47 ≠ 2:68. You must use "count on" or borrow 60 minutes, not 10 or 100.
Midnight: 11:50 pm to 00:30 am = 40 minutes, not 11 hours 20 minutes.

5Area Unit Conversion — The Squared Trap

When you convert a length, you multiply or divide by the linear scale factor. But area has two dimensions, so you must square the scale factor.

⚠️ The Most Common Exam Mistake

Students see "1 m = 100 cm" and write "1 m² = 100 cm²". This is wrong. Because area is length × width, both dimensions convert: 100 cm × 100 cm = 10,000 cm². So 1 m² = 10,000 cm².

📐 Area Conversion Table
1 cm² = 100 mm²    (because 10 × 10 = 100)
1 m² = 10,000 cm²    (because 100 × 100 = 10,000)
1 km² = 1,000,000 m²    (because 1000 × 1000 = 1,000,000)
Rule: Area scale factor = (linear scale factor)²
✏️ Worked Example 1 — m² to cm²
Question: Convert 3.5 m² to cm².
Scale factor: 1 m² = 10,000 cm²
Direction: m² → cm²: big to small → multiply
Calculation: 3.5 × 10,000 = 35,000
✅ 3.5 m² = 35,000 cm²
✏️ Worked Example 2 — cm² to m²
Question: Convert 75,000 cm² to m².
Scale factor: 1 m² = 10,000 cm²
Direction: cm² → m²: small to big → divide
Calculation: 75,000 ÷ 10,000 = 7.5
✅ 75,000 cm² = 7.5 m²

6Volume Unit Conversion — The Cubed Trap

Volume has three dimensions, so when converting units you must cube the linear scale factor — not square it, not use it directly.

⚠️ The Cubed Trap

1 m = 100 cm — but 1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³ (because 100 × 100 × 100 = 1,000,000). Students who write 1 m³ = 100 cm³ or 10,000 cm³ lose all the marks. Always cube the linear factor.

📐 Volume Conversion Table
1 cm³ = 1,000 mm³    (because 10³ = 1,000)
1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³    (because 100³ = 1,000,000)
1 km³ = 1,000,000,000 m³    (because 1000³ = 1 billion)
Rule: Volume scale factor = (linear scale factor)³
🔗 Reminder — Capacity Link

1 cm³ = 1 ml   ·   1000 cm³ = 1 litre   ·   1 m³ = 1,000,000 ml = 1,000 litres

✏️ Worked Example 1 — m³ to cm³
Question: Convert 2.5 m³ to cm³.
Scale factor: 1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³
Calculation: 2.5 × 1,000,000 = 2,500,000
✅ 2.5 m³ = 2,500,000 cm³
✏️ Worked Example 2 — cm³ to mm³
Question: Convert 4 cm³ to mm³.
Scale factor: 1 cm³ = 1,000 mm³
Calculation: 4 × 1,000 = 4,000
✅ 4 cm³ = 4,000 mm³

✏️ Worked Example — Miles to km
Question: A road sign shows 40 miles. Approximately how many km?
Rule: 5 miles ≈ 8 km → 1 mile ≈ 1.6 km
Calculation: 40 × 1.6 = 64 km    (or: 40 ÷ 5 × 8 = 64 km)
✅ 40 miles ≈ 64 km
✏️ Worked Example — Pints to litres
Question: A container holds 6 pints. Approximately how many litres?
Rule: 8 pints = 1 gallon ≈ 4.5 litres → 1 pint ≈ 4.5 ÷ 8 = 0.5625 litres
Approximate method: 6 pints ≈ 6 × 0.568 ≈ 3.4 litres
✅ 6 pints ≈ 3.4 litres (GL papers will give you the conversion factor)
