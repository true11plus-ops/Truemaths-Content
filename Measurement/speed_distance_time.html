01 The Formula Triangle

Cover the letter you want to find. The remaining two letters show the operation.

Cover D → S × T
D S T
Cover S → D ÷ T  |  Cover T → D ÷ S
Cover D → D = S × T
Cover S → S = D ÷ T
Cover T → T = D ÷ S
⚠ Units must match: If speed is in km/h, then distance must be in km and time must be in hours. Mixing units is the #1 cause of wrong answers.

02 Worked Example for Each Formula

2a. Finding Distance — D = S × T

Worked Example A
A car travels at 60 km/h for 2.5 hours. How far does it travel?
1
Identify: S = 60 km/h, T = 2.5 h, find D
2
Formula: D = S × T
3
Calculate: D = 60 × 2.5 = 150 kmDistance = 150 km

2b. Finding Speed — S = D ÷ T

Worked Example B
A car travels 150 km in 2.5 hours. What is its average speed?
1
Identify: D = 150 km, T = 2.5 h, find S
2
Formula: S = D ÷ T
3
Calculate: S = 150 ÷ 2.5 = 60 km/hSpeed = 60 km/h

2c. Finding Time — T = D ÷ S

Worked Example C
A train travels 180 km at 60 km/h. How long does the journey take? Give your answer in hours and minutes.
1
Identify: D = 180 km, S = 60 km/h, find T
2
Formula: T = D ÷ S
3
Calculate: T = 180 ÷ 60 = 3.0 hours
4
Convert to h and min: 3.0 h = 3 hours 0 minutesTime = 3 hours 0 minutes

03 The Decimal Trap

Time is almost always given in minutes in GL problems, but the formula needs hours. Converting incorrectly is the most common way to lose marks on this topic.

15 min = 0.25 h 15 ÷ 60 = 0.25
30 min = 0.5 h 30 ÷ 60 = 0.5
45 min = 0.75 h 45 ÷ 60 = 0.75
Any mins ÷ 60 = hours General rule
Decimal Trap — Full Worked Example
A cyclist rides at 18 km/h for 40 minutes. How far does she travel?
✗
Wrong: D = 18 × 40 = 720 km
Error: used minutes as if they were hours. 720 km on a bicycle is impossible!
1
Convert first: 40 min ÷ 60 = 0.667 h
2
Apply formula: D = 18 × 0.667 = 12 kmDistance = 12 km ✓
Sanity check: At 18 km/h you cover 18 km in a full hour. In 40 minutes (⅔ of an hour) you'd cover ⅔ × 18 = 12 km. ✓
Easy
Convert 30 minutes to decimal hours. (e.g. type 0.5)
Convert 45 minutes to decimal hours.

04 Unit Conversion Chain

When the speed unit and the answer unit don't match, you must convert at either the start or end. Always label each step.

Worked Example — km/h → metres
A train travels at 90 km/h. How many metres does it travel in 5 minutes?
1
Convert time to hours: 5 min ÷ 60 = 1/12 h ≈ 0.0833 h
2
Find distance in km: D = 90 × (5/60) = 90 × 0.0833 = 7.5 km
3
Convert km → metres: 7.5 × 1,000 = 7,500 mDistance = 7,500 metres
Unit Conversion Quick Reference km → m: × 1,000   |   m → km: ÷ 1,000
hours → minutes: × 60   |   minutes → hours: ÷ 60
minutes → seconds: × 60   |   hours → seconds: × 3,600
Intermediate

Advanced

Challenging

05 Average Speed

When a journey has multiple legs at different speeds, you cannot simply average the speeds. You must use total distance ÷ total time.

Why Averaging Speeds is WRONG
A car travels 120 km at 60 km/h, then 270 km at 90 km/h. What is the average speed for the whole journey?
Wrong approach: average the two speeds
(60 + 90) ÷ 2 = 75 km/h ✗
This gives 75 km/h — but the correct answer is different, because the car spent different amounts of time at each speed.
1
Total Distance: 120 + 270 = 390 km
2
Time for leg 1: T₁ = 120 ÷ 60 = 2 h
3
Time for leg 2: T₂ = 270 ÷ 90 = 3 h
4
Total Time: 2 + 3 = 5 h
5
Average Speed: 390 ÷ 5 = 78 km/hAverage Speed = 78 km/h (not 75!)
The Golden Rule Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time
NEVER = (Speed 1 + Speed 2) ÷ 2
Intermediate
A cyclist rides 100 km at 50 km/h, then 100 km at 100 km/h. What is the average speed for the whole journey? (km/h)
A runner runs 6 km at 12 km/h then 4 km at 8 km/h. What is her average speed? (km/h, to 2 d.p.)
Advanced
A car completes a 240 km journey at 80 km/h, then a 180 km return trip at 90 km/h. What is the average speed for the entire round trip? (to 2 d.p., km/h)

06 Two-Object Problems

When two objects move relative to each other, you calculate a relative speed — either the sum or the difference of their speeds.

6a. Moving TOWARD Each Other — Closing Speed = Sum of Speeds

Worked Example — Toward
Town A and Town B are 200 km apart. Car X leaves A at 60 km/h heading toward B. Car Y leaves B at 40 km/h heading toward A. How long before they meet?
← 200 km total distance →
Car X 60 km/h
→
←
Car Y 40 km/h
They are closing the gap at (60 + 40) = 100 km/h
1
Closing speed: 60 + 40 = 100 km/h
2
Time to meet: T = 200 ÷ 100 = 2 hoursThey meet after 2 hours.

6b. Moving in the SAME Direction — Relative Speed = Difference of Speeds

Worked Example — Same Direction
Car A travels at 80 km/h. Car B is 60 km ahead, traveling in the same direction at 50 km/h. How long until Car A catches Car B?
Car B is 60 km ahead →
Car A 80 km/h
→
Car B 50 km/h
→
A closes the gap at (80 − 50) = 30 km/h
1
Relative speed (closing): 80 − 50 = 30 km/h
2
Time to catch up: T = 60 ÷ 30 = 2 hoursCar A catches Car B after 2 hours.
Intermediate
Two trains start 300 km apart, heading toward each other. Train A travels at 70 km/h and Train B at 50 km/h. How long until they meet? (hours)
Car A travels at 90 km/h. Car B is 90 km ahead in the same direction at 60 km/h. How long until A catches B? (hours)
Advanced

07 The Missing-Leg Problem

A journey has two stages. You know the total time, plus the full details of one stage. You must find the speed or distance of the other stage — using the formula twice in sequence.

Worked Example — Find Speed of Leg 2
A journey takes 3 hours in total. In the first part, a car travels 120 km at 60 km/h. In the second part, it travels 90 km. What is the speed during the second part?
1
Time for Leg 1: T₁ = 120 ÷ 60 = 2 hours
2
Time for Leg 2: T₂ = Total − T₁ = 3 − 2 = 1 hour
3
Speed for Leg 2: S₂ = D₂ ÷ T₂ = 90 ÷ 1 = 90 km/hSpeed in second part = 90 km/h
Advanced
A total journey takes 4 hours. Leg 1: 150 km at 75 km/h. Leg 2 is at 60 km/h. What is the distance of Leg 2? (km)
A journey totals 5 hours. Leg 1 is 200 km at 80 km/h. Leg 2 is 135 km. What is the speed of Leg 2? (km/h)
Challenging
A car's total journey is 350 km in 4 hours. Leg 1 is 200 km at 80 km/h. What is the average speed for Leg 2 only? (km/h)

08 Converting m/s ↔ km/h

Standard Conversion m/s → km/h: multiply by 3.6
km/h → m/s: divide by 3.6

Why 3.6? Because 1 km/h = 1000 m ÷ 3600 s = 1/3.6 m/s. So 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h.
Worked Example — m/s to km/h
A sprinter runs at 15 m/s. What is this in km/h?
1
Apply rule: 15 × 3.6 = 54 km/h15 m/s = 54 km/h
Worked Example — km/h to m/s
A car travels at 72 km/h. What is this in m/s?
1
Apply rule: 72 ÷ 3.6 = 20 m/s72 km/h = 20 m/s

09 Estimation & Sanity Checking

The General Principle — Distance per Minute

You can always derive how far something travels per minute from its km/h speed:

1
Starting from S km/h: this means S km in 60 minutes.
2
Per minute: S ÷ 60 km per minute
3
Example: At 90 km/h → 90 ÷ 60 = 1.5 km per minute. In 5 minutes: 1.5 × 5 = 7.5 km ✓ (matches our unit chain example)
Using Estimation to Eliminate MCQ Options

Question: A car at 108 km/h — how far in 10 minutes?

E
Quick estimate: Round to 100 km/h. Per minute = 100÷60 ≈ 1.67 km. In 10 min ≈ 17 km.
✗
Options like 180 km (used 10 as hours!) or 1.8 km (forgot ×10) are immediately eliminated.
✓
Exact answer: 108 × (10/60) = 18 km. Our estimate of ≈17 km confirms this is in range. ✓
The 60 mph benchmark (derivable, not memorised): 60 mph ÷ 60 = 1 mile per minute. This works for ANY speed: speed (mph) ÷ 60 = miles per minute.
Easy

At 120 km/h, how many km does a vehicle travel per minute?
Intermediate
